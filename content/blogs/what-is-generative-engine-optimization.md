Six months ago, a client asked me why their top-ranking blog post got zero mentions in ChatGPT's answer to the exact same question.

Page 1 on Google. Rank 3, actually. Nowhere in the AI answer.

That's when it clicked for me: ranking and being cited are two different games now. And the second one has a name.

## What is GEO?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization.

It's the practice of structuring your content so AI systems like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Gemini can find it, understand it, and quote it in their generated answers.

Traditional SEO gets you a blue link. GEO gets you a citation inside an AI-written response, sometimes with no click at all.

I'd define it simply: if SEO optimizes for search engine crawlers and human readers, GEO optimizes for large language models that summarize, synthesize, and answer on your behalf.

The term picked up steam after a 2024 Princeton and Georgia Tech study (the paper that coined "GEO" in academic research) tested content tweaks across generative search tools and found some techniques boosted visibility in AI answers by over 40%. That's not a marginal gain. That's a different channel.

Some people call this AI Search Engines Optimization instead, others just say GEO. Same idea, different label depending on who's writing the report.

## Why AI search optimization matters right now

Google's AI Overviews now show up on a huge share of informational searches. I've watched click-through rates on my own tracked keywords drop by 15 to 30% the moment an AI Overview appeared above my listing, even at position 1.

ChatGPT alone processes hundreds of millions of prompts a week. Add Perplexity, Copilot, and Gemini, and you've got a meaningful slice of research and buying behavior happening inside a chat window, not a search results page.

Here's the part that matters for marketers: users don't scroll past the AI answer as often as they used to scroll past a featured snippet. If your brand isn't in that answer, you're invisible for that query, no click and no impression.

I started tracking this for my own site and for client accounts around mid-2025. Traffic from traditional blue links held steady. But branded search volume (people typing my name or my clients' brand names after seeing them referenced in an AI answer) started climbing. That's GEO working indirectly, through brand recall rather than a direct click.

## How AI search engines actually pick what to cite

Large language models don't crawl your site the way Googlebot does. Most pull from a mix of their training data, real-time retrieval, and indexes from partners like Bing.

From testing dozens of prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews, I've noticed a pattern in what gets pulled:

- Clear, direct answers within the first 100 words of a section
- Content structured with proper headers that mirror actual questions
- Original data, stats, or examples the model can't get anywhere else
- Pages with strong topical depth, not thin 300-word posts
- Sources the model already trusts (high authority domains, consistent citations elsewhere)

Notice what's missing from that list: keyword stuffing, meta tag tricks, backlink farms. Old-school manipulation tactics don't translate here. LLMs are trained to extract meaning, not match strings.

## How to optimize for AI search: 7 things that actually move the needle

I've run these on my own portfolio content and on client blogs. Here's what worked.

**1. Answer the question in the first two sentences.**
AI models extract snippets. If your answer is buried under three paragraphs of introduction, it won't get pulled. Put the direct answer immediately after the header, then expand.

**2. Use question-based subheadings.**
"How to optimize for AI search" as an H2 performs better than "Optimization strategies" because it mirrors how people actually prompt AI tools. I rewrote a client's FAQ section this way in late 2025 and their AI Overview appearances tripled within 6 weeks, based on Search Console's search appearance filter.

**3. Add original data.**
Nobody can cite a stat that already exists on 40 other sites. Run your own small survey, pull numbers from your own campaigns, or cite a specific study by name. I use real client results (with permission) instead of generic industry claims.

**4. Build topical depth.**
A cluster of 8 tightly connected articles on one subject outperforms 20 scattered ones. LLMs weigh entity relationships. Depth signals expertise.

**5. Mark up your content with schema.**
FAQ schema, Article schema, and Author schema give structured signals that both search engines and AI crawlers can parse cleanly. It's not optional anymore, it's baseline hygiene.

**6. Get cited on other authoritative sites.**
LLMs weigh source trust heavily. A mention on a respected industry publication does more for your AI visibility than 10 low-quality backlinks.

**7. Write like a person who's done the work.**
Specific numbers, named tools, actual outcomes. Generic AI-sounding fluff gets deprioritized by models trained to detect low-signal content. Ironic, but true.

## GEO vs SEO vs AEO: how they fit together

I get asked this constantly, so here's the short version. If you're still wondering what is Generative Engine Optimization compared to the other two, think of it this way: SEO is the foundation, AEO is the shortcut, GEO is the conversation.

SEO gets you found in traditional search results. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) targets featured snippets and voice assistants. GEO targets generative AI tools that write full answers using multiple sources.

They overlap heavily. A well-structured, authoritative, schema-rich page tends to perform across all three. I don't treat them as separate content strategies anymore, I treat GEO as the newest layer on top of solid SEO fundamentals.

## What I'd tell you if you're starting today

Pick 3 to 5 of your highest-traffic pages. Rewrite the opening of each core section to answer the implied question directly within the first sentence or two. Add one genuinely original stat or example per page. Add FAQ schema.

Then track your brand mentions in AI Overviews and ChatGPT responses manually, weekly, for a month. Search Console doesn't fully separate this data yet, so you're doing some of this by hand for now.

Google, OpenAI, and Perplexity are all still changing how they source and cite content. What worked in my tests in early 2026 might shift by the time you read this. Treat GEO as an ongoing practice, not a one-time fix.

## Quick recap: what is GEO in one line

If you only remember one thing: GEO is writing and structuring content so AI answer engines can lift it directly into their responses, with clear answers, real data, and structure that mirrors how people actually ask questions. That's the whole game right now.
